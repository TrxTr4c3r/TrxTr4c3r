# Confirmation Bias and Misleading On-Chain Data

<span style={{ fontFamily: 'var(--font-space-mono), monospace', fontSize: 13, opacity: 0.7 }}>`caution`  ·  Level 1  ·  confirmation-bias · misleading-data · methodology · crypto-investigation</span>

## Core idea
One of the biggest risks to a crypto investigator is confirmation bias fed by misleading information. Because it is so easy to follow assets from one address to the next, you can build a convincing but wrong narrative if you do not weigh all the context. The chain shows movement, not meaning, and movement alone will lead you down false paths.

## When to use
Whenever a trail seems to confirm a theory quickly and cleanly. Treat that as a prompt to seek disconfirming context, not as proof.
