# Advanced Attribution Techniques

<span style={{ fontFamily: 'var(--font-space-mono), monospace', fontSize: 13, opacity: 0.7 }}>`checklist`</span>

## Core idea
Six supplementary techniques that push attribution beyond basic graph tracing, exploiting exchange chokepoints, timing, associates, holdings, vanity addresses, and cross-chain OPSEC gaps.

## Components
- Exchange tracing: funds reach exchanges for liquidation; identifying the exchange lets law enforcement request KYC records via legal process.
- Time correlation: match on-chain timestamps to off-chain events (social posts, incident timelines); timezone patterns also provide profiling data.
- Social graph analysis: recurring counterparties early in a wallet's life often include associates with weaker OPSEC than the primary subject.
- Token holdings: niche DeFi positions or early-access NFTs narrow identity significantly when cross-referenced with public discussion.
- Vanity address analysis: vanity addresses are generated deliberately; the tool and creation time can sometimes be matched against public records or forum posts.
- Bridge and cross-chain: a subject may have strong OPSEC on Ethereum but weaker habits on an alternative chain they bridged to.

## When to use
When basic transaction-graph tracing stalls and you need additional angles to identify or narrow down a subject.

## Example
Noticing a subject bridged funds to a less-used chain and finding they reused an identifiable pattern there, exploiting the weaker cross-chain OPSEC.
