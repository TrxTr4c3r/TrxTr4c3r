# The Scope Triangle

<span style={{ fontFamily: 'var(--font-space-mono), monospace', fontSize: 13, opacity: 0.7 }}>`concept`</span>

## Core idea
Three variables shape every investigation, time, data, and resources. They are interdependent: change one and the others must flex. Pretending otherwise is how cases run over.

## Components
- Time: how deep you can go. 24 hours buys the highest-value pivots and likely endpoints; a week buys multiple laundering paths, OSINT correlation, and robust validation.
- Data: what is even possible. A single wallet address yields a partial picture; logs, communications, screenshots, domains, and transaction history yield a rich one.
- Resources: tools, access, expertise, legal powers, team capacity. Some cases need subpoenas or RFIs, some need specialist crypto analysts, some need only open-source tooling.

## When to use
When scoping a case and managing client expectations, to explain why more data demands more time or resources, and why less time means less scope unless resources increase.

## Example
The law of the triangle: more data needs more time or more resources; less time means less scope, or more resources. If a client adds chains to trace but keeps the deadline fixed, either resources must rise or scope must shrink.
