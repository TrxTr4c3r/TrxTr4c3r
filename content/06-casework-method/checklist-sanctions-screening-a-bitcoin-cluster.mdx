# Sanctions Screening a Bitcoin Cluster

<span style={{ fontFamily: 'var(--font-space-mono), monospace', fontSize: 13, opacity: 0.7 }}>`checklist`  ·  Level 2  ·  bitcoin · sanctions · compliance · ofac · ofsi · screening</span>

## Core idea
Before publishing any Bitcoin cluster findings, check the addresses against sanctions lists. For commercial/compliance work, a sanctions hit is itself the headline finding -- it does not need further tracing to be actionable.

## Components
- OFAC SDN list -- contains sanctioned crypto addresses; U.S. Treasury maintains machine-readable formats.
- UK OFSI list -- UK sanctions, including crypto addresses published alongside entity designations.
- EU consolidated list -- partial coverage; national regulators extend it.

## When to use
Before publishing cluster findings, and as a standard step in commercial due-diligence / compliance screening of Bitcoin (or other) addresses.

## Example
A due-diligence check on a counterparty's disclosed wallets returns an OFAC SDN match -- that hit is the deliverable finding, no onward tracing required to justify declining the arrangement.
