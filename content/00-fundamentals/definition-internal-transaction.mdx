# Internal Transactions

<span style={{ fontFamily: 'var(--font-space-mono), monospace', fontSize: 13, opacity: 0.7 }}>`definition`</span>

## Core idea
An internal transaction is a transfer that originates from within a smart contract rather than directly from a wallet. A smart contract is an automated workflow for money, so when you interact with one it can move ETH on your behalf. These appear on a separate explorer tab and are often where a funding source is hidden.

## When to use
When a wallet appears to have no normal incoming transaction explaining its balance: check the internal transactions tab, since the funding may have come out of a contract such as a mixer.

## Example
An address funded by Tornado Cash shows the incoming value under internal transactions, because the withdrawal was initiated from the Tornado Cash contract.
