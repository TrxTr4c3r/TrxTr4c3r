# Mixing / Tumbling (CoinJoin)

<span style={{ fontFamily: 'var(--font-space-mono), monospace', fontSize: 13, opacity: 0.7 }}>`concept`  ·  Level reference  ·  money-laundering · mixer · coinjoin · tumbler · obfuscation · typology</span>

## Core idea
Pools multiple users' funds into a single equal-value, multi-party transaction, or routes through a custodial tumbler, so outputs can't be matched back to specific inputs.

## When to use
Equal-value, multi-input/multi-output transaction structure; treat downstream funds as unattributable past this point absent other evidence.

## Example
Equal-value inputs from many parties produce equal-value outputs, severing the link between who sent and who received.
