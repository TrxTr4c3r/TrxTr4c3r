# Why People Use Mixers and Tumblers

<span style={{ fontFamily: 'var(--font-space-mono), monospace', fontSize: 13, opacity: 0.7 }}>`concept`</span>

## Core idea
Mixers serve both protective and illicit purposes, which is why they are treated as dual-use and high-risk. The same service some use to protect financial privacy is used by others to launder proceeds and evade controls, so mixer interaction is an indicator rather than proof of crime on its own.

## Components
- Protect financial privacy
- Avoid surveillance
- Hide illicit funds and launder proceeds
- Evade sanctions and trade restrictions
- Bypass AML and CFT controls
- Facilitate ransomware payments, darknet markets, terrorist financing, and other cybercrimes

## When to use
Use when assessing intent, framing a risk narrative, or explaining why mixer interaction raises suspicion without being conclusive in isolation.
